Understanding Surgical Sutures

Surgical sutures, commonly known as stitches, are medical devices used to hold body tissues together after an injury or surgery. They play a crucial role in wound healing by approximating tissue edges until natural healing processes provide sufficient strength.

Modern sutures are engineered with precision to provide optimal tensile strength, flexibility, and biocompatibility for various surgical applications.

Types of Sutures

Absorbable Sutures

  • Break down naturally in the body over time
  • No removal required - reduces patient discomfort
  • Ideal for internal tissues and deep wounds
  • Common materials: Polyglactin, Poliglecaprone, Polyglycolic acid (PGA), Polylactic acid (PLA), Polydioxanone (PDS) etc

Non-Absorbable Sutures

  • Remain in body permanently or require removal
  • Provide long-term structural support
  • Used for skin closure, tendons, and vessels
  • Common materials: Polymide, Polypropylene, Nylon, Silk etc

Key Features

Biocompatibility

Minimal tissue reaction and excellent compatibility with human tissues for reduced inflammation.

Tensile Strength

High strength retention to withstand physiological stresses during the healing process.

Easy Handling

Excellent knot security, flexibility, and minimal memory for precise surgical control.

Infection Control

Advanced coatings and materials that reduce the risk of surgical site infections.

Our Products

High-quality surgical sutures designed for optimal performance and patient outcomes.

Absorbable Sutures

Temporary wound support that breaks down naturally in the body over time, eliminating the need for removal.

  • Natural absorption over 60-90 days
  • Made from polyglycolic acid & polyglactin
  • No removal required - reduces infection risk
  • Ideal for internal tissues, General Closure, Orthopaedics and Obstetrics/Gynacology surgery

Non-Absorbable Sutures

Permanent wound support designed for long-term tissue reinforcement where removal is required.

  • Long-term permanent wound support
  • Made from nylon, polyester & polypropylene
  • Superior knot security & minimal memory
  • Ideal for skin closures since they do not exhibit tissue adherence
