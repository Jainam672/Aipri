Understanding Surgical Sutures

Sutures are essential medical devices used to hold tissues together after an injury or surgery, playing a crucial role until natural healing provides sufficient strength.

Absorbable Sutures

Designed to break down naturally in the body over time. Ideal for internal tissues, eliminating the need for removal.

Non-Absorbable Sutures

Provide long-term structural support and require removal post-healing. Commonly used for skin closure and tendons.
